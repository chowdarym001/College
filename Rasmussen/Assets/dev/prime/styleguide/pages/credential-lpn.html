What will I be able to do when Im done?

  • Sit for the NCLEX-PN Exam

    Prep work for the national certification exam, NCLEX-PN, is built into every course. That means you can confidently earn the licensure you need to become a confident LPN.

  • Be a Practicing LPN

    Our graduates are able to work for leaders in the following industries: assisted living, clinics, home health care, outpatient surgery centers and rehabilitation centers.

  • Strong Earning Potential

    According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), earning a degree can improve income potential. The yearly projected salary range for licensed practical and vocational nurses is between $32,040 - $43,170.*

What skills will I gain?

  • Familiarity with healthcare settings

    We hold clinicals in different settings to ensure youre comfortable, knowledgeable, and adaptablewherever youre working.

  • Hands-on clinical training

    Providing preventative, therapeutic and maintenance care to patients demands your education be hands-on. Thats exactly what you can expect.

  • Basic patient care

    Youll become proficient in taking temperatures and blood pressures and dressing woundskey foundational nursing skills.

  • Expertise in gathering health data

    Knowing how to properly observe patients and chart and report changes in their condition is another reason LPNs are critical to patient care.

What type of classes will I take?

LPN Diploma

Sample Courses

  • Fundamentals of Practical Nursing

    PRN 1192 // On Campus // 6 Credits

    Focuses on the fundamental concepts and nursing abilities required to meet basic health and wellness needs. Includes a theory, laboratory and clinical component.

  • Introduction to Practical Nursing

    PRN 1086 // On Campus // 2 Credits

    Emphasizes key concepts of practical nursing, including expectations and scope of practice in diverse healthcare settings. Includes aspects of patient-centered care based upon evidence and quality.

  • Family Nursing

    PRN 1932 // On Campus // 6 Credits

    Imparts the knowledge, skills and attitudes required to function in the appropriate role of the practical nurse in a family health clinic, pediatric clinical setting or similar environment. Consists of both a theory and clinical component.
